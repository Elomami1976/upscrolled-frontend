1. TikTok — The Industry Giant

Best for: Entertainment, viral content, trends

Pros:

  • Largest user base (1+ billion active users)
  • Most sophisticated recommendation algorithm
  • Extensive editing tools and effects
  • Huge potential for viral reach
  • Strong creator monetization programs

Cons:

  • Can feel overwhelming/chaotic
  • Algorithm changes frequently
  • Privacy concerns in some regions
  • Downloads include watermarks

Who should use it: Anyone wanting maximum exposure or entertainment variety. Essential for creators targeting Gen Z audiences.

2. Instagram Reels — Social-First Video

Best for: Leveraging existing followers, lifestyle content

Pros:

  • Integrated with Instagram's social features
  • Great for building a cohesive brand
  • Access to Instagram's shopping features
  • Cross-posting to Facebook
  • Strong for fashion, beauty, lifestyle niches

Cons:

  • Algorithm favors established accounts
  • No native download option
  • Shorter maximum video length
  • Less organic discovery than TikTok

Who should use it: Creators with existing Instagram followings, lifestyle brands, businesses with visual products.

3. YouTube Shorts — Long-Term Discoverability

Best for: Evergreen content, building a channel

Pros:

  • Videos can be discovered years later
  • Integrates with YouTube's ecosystem
  • Strong monetization (once eligible)
  • Great for educational content
  • Can drive traffic to longer videos

Cons:

  • Shorter format (60 seconds max)
  • Less "fun" editing tools than TikTok
  • Monetization requires meeting thresholds
  • Algorithm still maturing

Who should use it: Educators, how-to creators, anyone wanting content to stay discoverable long-term.

4. Snapchat Spotlight — Privacy-Focused

Best for: Younger audiences, ephemeral content

Pros:

  • Strong privacy features
  • Large Gen Z user base
  • AR filters and lenses
  • Anonymous submissions possible
  • Cash bonuses for viral content

Cons:

  • Smaller overall audience
  • Limited discovery features
  • Videos can't be downloaded
  • Less creator tools

Who should use it: Younger users prioritizing privacy, creators experimenting with AR content.
